A wireframe is a visual representation of a user interface, stripped of any visual design or branding elements. It helps designers and stakeholders come together to understand the app's functionality and layout before diving into the intricate details of the design.

In this post, I want to detail my workflow of designing the wireframe for the site.


Choose Your Tools

There are many wireframing tools available, such as Sketch, Figma, Balsamiq, and Adobe XD. I chose to use a relatively new software called Uizard, which provides me with the same tools as other sites, with some extra features.

  • User registration and login
  • Searching for a game
  • Adding a game to their archive
  • Rating and reviewing a game


Move to Digital

Once I was satisfied with the sketches, I recreated them in uizard. This helped me create a clearer picture of the layout and functionality.

Gather Feedback

Once the first draft was completed, I shared my wireframes with potential users to gauge its effectiveness. Their feedback helped refine the design and I'm still making adjustments.


Next Steps

Once my wireframe is finalized, I'll move on to creating a high-fidelity design. This will include colors, typography, images, and other branding elements.
