In the vast realm of digital technology, I've often found myself immersed in the intangible, abstract world of code and virtual interfaces. But today, I'm venturing into a new frontier: the realm of physical computing. Welcome to what is the beginning of a series of posts related to physical computing and alternative interfaces. I'll be detailing my journey of setting up my Arduino, marking my initial foray into this exciting domain.

The Basics of Electricity

Electrical energy flows through conductors, like wires, and can be converted into other forms of energy, such as light or sound. This conversion is done using electrical transducers. These transducers can either convert other forms of energy into electrical energy (called sensors) or convert electrical energy into other forms (known as actuators).

Building the Circuit

With the basics in place, I moved on to building my first interactive circuit. The components I used were simple: a switch, a resistor, and an LED. In this setup, the Arduino served merely as the power source. The idea was straightforward – when a user presses the button, the LED lights up!

Key Information

  • Current: The amount of electrical charge flowing past a specific point in a circuit.
  • Voltage: The difference in energy between one point in a circuit and another.
  • Resistance: How much a component resists the flow of electrical energy.

Reflection

Building this circuit was super fun. I got hands-on experience with breadboards and I'm slowly beginning to understand the importance of each component in the circuit. Seeing the LED light up was a simple interaction, but it's made me excited to see what else I can make.
