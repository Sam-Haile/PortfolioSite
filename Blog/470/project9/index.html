The Motorized Pinwheel project delves into the world of Arduino and motor control. It's a project that not only introduces the basics of motor control using an Arduino but also highlights the intricacies involved in managing high current and voltage loads. Let's dive into the project and explore what we've learned.


Project Overview


The primary objective of the Motorized Pinwheel project is to get the Arduino to spin a colorful pinwheel using a motor. While it might sound straightforward, controlling motors with an Arduino presents its own set of challenges. For starters, motors require more current than the Arduino’s output pins can supply. Additionally, motors can generate their own current through a process called induction, which can potentially damage the circuit if not accounted for.


Key Components and Their Roles

  • TransistorsThese are solid-state devices that play a crucial role in this project. Transistors allow us to control high current and high voltage power sources from the low current output of the Arduino. Essentially, they act as digital switches. When voltage is provided to one of the transistor’s pins (the gate), it closes the circuit between the other two pins (the source and drain).
  • Diodes:Diodes are polarized components that allow current to flow in one direction. In this project, a diode is used in parallel with the motor to ensure that any back-voltage generated by the motor doesn't damage the circuit. The diode ensures that this reverse voltage passes through it, protecting the rest of the circuit.
  • Motor:The heart of the project. Motors require more current and sometimes a higher voltage than what the Arduino can provide. When a motor starts moving or has a heavy load attached, it draws as much current as it can. The Arduino's digital pins can only provide up to 40 milliamps (mA), which is much less than what most motors require.

Key Takeaways

Project 9 offers valuable insights into the challenges of managing high current and voltage loads and provides practical solutions to overcome these challenges:

  • Understanding Motor Behavior:When energy is no longer supplied to a motor, it continues to spin due to inertia. This spinning can generate a voltage in the opposite direction of the supplied current. This reverse voltage, or back-voltage, can damage components like transistors if not managed properly.
  • Importance of Diodes: The project emphasizes the importance of diodes in protecting circuits from back-voltage. By allowing electricity to flow in only one direction, diodes safeguard the rest of the circuit from potential damage.
