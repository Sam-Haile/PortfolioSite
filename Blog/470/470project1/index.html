Imagine touching a plant and, like magic, it plays a musical note. It sounds like a scene from a fantasy movie, but with a touch of creativity, some technical know-how, and the versatility of Arduino, it's entirely possible. This blog post will take you through my process of how I turned an ordinary plant into an interactive music-playing device.


Components


Statement of Experience Goal

The project's foundation was built on the principles of capacitive sensing, which is a way of detecting touch by measuring changes in capacitance. I researched various projects and experiments, but couldn't find many resources that were similar to what i wanted to create.

Circuit Design

The circuit design was pretty straightforward. The touch sensor was connected to the Arduino, which in turn was connected to the speaker. I used an online tool, Tinkercad, to create a clear and concise schematic of the setup. This tool was invaluable in planning and documenting the circuit design.


Coding the Arduino

The code was written in the Arduino IDE. It was designed to detect changes in capacitance from the touch sensor and translate those changes into musical notes. The CapacitiveSensor library was a crucial technical reference for this part of the project.

Issues, Discoveries, and Results

One of the biggest challenges I faced when working on this project was the capacitive touch sensors. They could not directly “record” changes in capacitance like I had originally assumed. To overcome this problem I used two fingers, one to cause a change in capacitance of the plant, and the other to signal which touch sensor was touched.

Results

This project demonstrated that with a bit of ingenuity, we can create interactions that are not only functional but also fun. The plant music player showcases the endless possibilities of Arduino. The potential for integrating technology with the natural environment opens up a world of possibilities for interactive design and experiences.
